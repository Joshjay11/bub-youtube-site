# BUB AI Prompts — Complete Reference Guide

These 8 prompts help you THINK through your video, not write it for you. Phase 1 is for before you draft. Phase 2 is for after you have script text.

---

## Recommended Flows

**Quick Path** (most videos): 3A → 3D → Write → 3F → 3G
**Deep Path** (important videos): 3A → 3B → 3C → 3D → Write → 3F → 3G → 3H
**Polish Only** (second draft): 3F → 3G

---

## Phase 1: Pre-Writing Prompts

### 3A — Find the Angle

**What it does:** Generates 5 fresh angles for your video topic that your audience hasn't heard before.

**When to use it:** After you have a validated idea (Idea Scorecard score of 30+) but before you've committed to a specific angle. This is usually the first AI prompt you run.

**How to use it:** The topic, audience, and channel perspective fields auto-fill from your upstream work. Add the "obvious take" — what every other creator says about this topic. The AI needs to know what to avoid.

**What to do with the output:** Don't pick the first angle that sounds clever. Look for the one that makes you think "I haven't seen that before AND I could actually make that video." Copy the winning angle into your Framing Worksheet.

**Good output:** 5 genuinely different angles where at least 2 make you think "I haven't seen that before." Hooks should be specific, not generic.

**Red flags:** All 5 angles are variations of the same idea. Hooks are vague ("discover the truth about..."). Angles require expertise you don't have.

---

### 3B — Find the Cross-Disciplinary Connection

**What it does:** Finds unexpected connections between your topic and completely different fields — psychology, history, science, economics.

**When to use it:** After 3A, when you have an angle but want to make it more original. This is what separates "another video on this topic" from "a video nobody else could make."

**How to use it:** Just needs your topic. The AI searches across domains for surprising connections.

**What to do with the output:** The best connection becomes your 35% Pivot. If psychology explains why your tech topic works the way it does, that's your reframe at the 1/3 mark.

**Good output:** At least one connection that surprises YOU. Connections should be specific, not surface-level analogies.

**Red flags:** Connections are surface-level. "It's like..." comparisons that don't add insight.

---

### 3C — Generate Counter-Arguments

**What it does:** Finds the strongest possible objections to your thesis so you can address them before your commenters do.

**When to use it:** After you've committed to an angle (3A) and optionally found a cross-disciplinary connection (3B). Run this before outlining so you can build counter-argument responses into your structure.

**How to use it:** Paste your thesis from the Framing Worksheet. The stronger and more specific your thesis, the better the counter-arguments.

**What to do with the output:** The counter-argument that makes you most uncomfortable is the one you MUST address in your script. Add it to your Research Keeper and plan where in the structure it goes (usually Micro-Act 2 or Escalation).

**Good output:** At least one counter-argument that makes you uncomfortable. That's the one you need to address.

**Red flags:** None of the counter-arguments challenge your position. The AI is going easy on you.

---

### 3D — Outline from Research

**What it does:** Takes everything you've built upstream (idea, scores, research, framing) and turns it into a structured video outline using the BUB beat map.

**When to use it:** After you've completed the Idea Validator, Research, and Framing Worksheet. This is the bridge between research and writing. The more upstream work you've done, the better this outline will be.

**How to use it:** The research notes field auto-fills with your full Running Brief — all your upstream work compiled. Add your target length and video type.

**What to do with the output:** This is your starting point for the Script Canvas, not your final structure. Copy it into the Script Canvas sections, then edit heavily. The outline gives you bones; you add the voice.

**Good output:** A clear outline where each section builds on the previous one, with specific data points and smooth transitions.

**Red flags:** Sections feel disconnected. The pivot doesn't actually reframe anything. Transitions are generic.

---

### 3E — Hook Variants

**What it does:** Generates 5 different hooks using 5 different techniques for the first 10 seconds of your video.

**When to use it:** If you've already used the Hook Writer on the Structure page, this is a second pass — useful when you've refined your angle since the first hook generation. Also useful if you want more options.

**How to use it:** Topic, angle, and audience belief auto-fill. Add your working title if you have one.

**What to do with the output:** Compare these with whatever hooks you generated on the Structure page. Pick the strongest, paste into the Hook Writer, and score it.

**Good output:** 5 genuinely different hooks where at least 2 make you want to keep watching. Written for spoken delivery, not essays.

**Red flags:** All hooks sound the same. They start with "I" or "So." They're longer than 3 sentences.

## Phase 2: Post-Writing Prompts

### 3F — Script Audit

**What it does:** AI reads your full script draft and audits it for retention problems — drop-off points, pacing issues, hook-to-body disconnect, missing pattern interrupts.

**When to use it:** After you have a complete first draft in the Script Canvas. Don't run this on a partial draft — the AI needs the full picture to evaluate structure and pacing.

**How to use it:** Paste your full script from the Script Canvas. The thesis and hook are included for comparison.

**What to do with the output:** Address every problem the AI flags, in order of severity. The hook and pivot issues are most critical. Pacing issues are second. Polish issues are last.

**Good output:** Specific, actionable feedback pointing to exact moments in your script. Each problem comes with a concrete fix.

**Red flags:** Vague feedback like "the middle could be stronger." No specific sections referenced.

---

### 3G — Compression Check

**What it does:** Takes wordy or academic sections of your script and rewrites them into tight, conversational delivery. Gives you two versions: tight and ultra-tight.

**When to use it:** After the Script Audit (3F), on specific sections that feel too long or too academic. Don't run this on your entire script — pick the 2-3 sections that need tightening.

**How to use it:** Paste the specific section that feels too long or too formal.

**What to do with the output:** The "tight" version is usually right. The "ultra-tight" version is for when you're over word count and need to cut aggressively. Pick one, then read it aloud to make sure it sounds like you.

**Good output:** Two versions that preserve all key information but sound like someone talking, not writing.

**Red flags:** The rewrite loses key information. Sounds like a textbook summary. Sentences are all the same length.

---

### 3H — AI Output Quality Scorecard

**What it does:** Scores any AI-generated structural output (outline, hook set, beat map) against 8 quality criteria before you use it in your script.

**When to use it:** Anytime you've generated something with 3D (outline) or 3E (hooks) and you're not sure if it's good enough to build on. This is your quality gate for AI output.

**How to use it:** Paste the AI output you want to evaluate.

**What to do with the output:** If it scores 30+/40, use it with voice edits. If 20-29, heavy editing needed. Below 20, re-prompt with more context rather than trying to fix bad bones.

**Good output:** Honest scoring with specific explanations. At least 2 criteria should score below 3.

**Red flags:** All scores are 4-5 with vague justifications. No specific critique.

---

## Tips

- **Run prompts in order within each phase.** 3A informs 3B which informs 3C which informs 3D. Skipping around gives worse results.
- **The auto-fill is your friend.** Fields pre-populate from your upstream work. The more you've done on the Idea Validator and Research pages, the better every prompt output will be.
- **Always edit the output.** AI gives you structure and angles. Your voice, personality, and judgment go in after.
- **Use the Thinking Partner alongside prompts.** If you're unsure about an AI output, ask the Thinking Partner to evaluate it before building on it.
